[A] Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data of Jenson Cycles USA using MySQL. The objective is to derive business insights related to store performance, customer behavior, product demand, and staff efficiency through advanced SQL queries.

The project demonstrates practical application of SQL in solving real-world retail business problems.

[B] Business Problem

The company faced challenges in:

Identifying top-performing stores and products

Tracking cumulative product sales over time

Finding high-value customers

Measuring staff performance

Detecting non-selling inventory

Without structured analysis, decision-making was inefficient.

[C] Tools & Technologies

MySQL

MySQL Workbench

Relational Database Concepts

Advanced SQL (Window Functions, Subqueries, EXISTS)

[D] Dataset Description

The dataset contains the following tables:

Customers

Orders

Order Items

Products

Categories

Stores

Staff

The data represents multi-store retail transactions across different product categories.

[E] Key Analysis Performed

Total products sold by each store

Cumulative quantity sold per product over time

Highest total sales product per category

Customer who spent the most

Highest-priced product in each category

Total orders per store

Staff members with no sales

Top 3 most sold products

Median product price

Products never ordered

Staff with above-average sales

Customers who ordered from all product categories

[F] Key Insights

Baldwin Bikes recorded the highest total quantity sold.

A small number of products contributed to a large share of total sales.

Some products were never ordered, indicating low demand.

Sales performance varied significantly across staff members.

Certain customers contributed disproportionately high revenue.

[G] Project Outcomes

Strengthened practical SQL and analytical skills

[H] Limitations
